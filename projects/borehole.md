Families in the Ibex Compound in Katete, Zambia, faced a critical water
shortage after a prolonged drought left the community's few water sources
unreliable and often contaminated. Children and women were walking miles
each day, carrying heavy containers, just for a sip of water.

### The Process

After months of sharing the community's need with supporters, the project
was fully funded. Our team traveled to Ibex Compound to share the news in
person with the compound's chairperson before beginning work on the
borehole.

### The Results

Watch below as the chairperson learns that the community's borehole is fully
funded — his reaction speaks for the whole village. A reliable source of
clean water means women and children will no longer have to walk miles each
day just to fetch it.
